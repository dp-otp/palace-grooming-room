Your shop at a glance — money, alerts, and daily summary

Stats, money, alerts
1
The 5 stat tiles at the top

These update in real time — no need to refresh. Week Revenue = total money you've confirmed since Monday. Payments = how many payments you confirmed today. Today's Revenue = how much money came in today. Staff On = how many barbers are marked as working right now. Active Discounts = how many discount codes are currently valid for customers to use. If a payment comes in while you're looking at the dashboard, the numbers update instantly.

2
Daily Briefing

A quick morning summary that refreshes every time you open the panel. Shows you: how much you made yesterday, how many bookings are on the books, which barbers are working, and your week total so far. Think of it as your "start of day" snapshot so you know where things stand before the first customer walks in.

3
Revenue Goal

Set a daily target (e.g. £500) and a progress bar fills up as payments come in. Pick from presets (£200, £300, £500, £750, £1000) or type your own number. The bar shows what percentage of your goal you've hit today. It can go over 100% if you smash your target. The goal stays the same every day until you change it — only the progress resets each morning.

4
Smart Alerts

These are warnings that pop up when something needs your attention. You'll see alerts for: Payments waiting — someone paid but you haven't confirmed it yet. Low stock — a product is running out. Unassigned commissions — a payment couldn't be auto-linked to a barber. Shop showing as closed during your opening hours. Each alert has a link that takes you straight to where you can fix it.

5
Shop Status — Open / Closed / Holiday / Emergency

Toggle Shop opens or closes your shop on the website. When you manually toggle it, the system respects your choice for 4 hours before the auto-scheduler can change it. Holiday Mode blocks all bookings and shows a holiday message on the website — use this when you're away. Emergency Close shuts everything down and stays shut until you manually reopen — useful for power cuts, flooding, or any emergency. The auto-scheduler normally opens your shop at your opening time and closes it at closing time, running in the background. But it always respects your manual toggles.

6
Cash Panel

For walk-ins or anyone paying cash. Tap a preset amount (£5, £10, £20, £50, £100) or type a custom amount. It instantly logs a confirmed payment — the money shows up in your revenue straight away. It also triggers all the background automation: updates stock if products are linked, queues a review request if there's a matching booking, and assigns commission to the barber. You'll see a green flash as confirmation.

7
Revenue Hero Card

The big card showing your total revenue, split into Card payments (bank transfers from the pay page) and Cash (what you logged manually). The trend arrow compares this week to last week — green arrow up means you're doing better, red arrow down means less than last week. Average spend tells you what the typical customer pays. The hourly chart shows when your busiest hours are, so you can plan staffing.

Pro Tip

The "Insights Strip" beneath the hero card shows things like "Busiest hour: 2pm" or "Cash is 60% of revenue" — auto-generated from this week's data. Glance at it to spot trends without digging into reports.

Payment Feed

Where you see and manage customer payments
▼
A payment's journey from customer to confirmed
Customer pays by card
→

Auto-confirmed
1
How payments appear here

When a customer goes through the pay page, selects their services, and completes the payment, a record appears in your feed instantly. It shows as "Pending" — meaning the customer says they paid, but you haven't verified it yet. Each payment shows: a reference code, a verify code, the amount, what services they paid for, and when they paid.

2
The 3 tabs

Pending — payments waiting for you to check and confirm. The yellow badge shows how many. This is where you spend most of your time. Confirmed — payments you've already approved. All — everything, including ones you rejected.

3
Confirming a payment

Card payments via Stripe are confirmed automatically. For cash payments, tap "Confirm" to verify the amount. Once confirmed, the payment counts towards your revenue and triggers all the automations (stock tracking, review requests, commissions).

4
What happens automatically when you confirm

Three things happen in the background the instant you confirm:

Stock updates — if you've set up products linked to the services they paid for (e.g. "Styling Gel" linked to "Skin Fade"), the stock count goes down automatically.

Review request — the system tries to find the customer's phone number from their booking and queues up a Google review request for you to send via WhatsApp.

Commission — the system works out which barber did the job (from the booking) and calculates their cut based on the rate you've set.

5
Rejecting a payment

If someone claims they paid but the money never arrived, or it looks fraudulent, tap "Reject". Rejected payments don't count towards anything — no revenue, no automation. They stay visible in the "All" tab with a red badge so you have a record.

Important

The automatic features (stock, reviews, commissions) work by matching the payment to a booking from the same day with the same service. This works great for customers who booked online. For walk-ins who didn't book, there's nothing to match — so you may need to manually assign the barber for commissions and manually enter the phone for review requests.

Revenue Tracking

See all the money that's come in, by week or month
▼
1
What counts as revenue

Only payments you've confirmed count. Pending payments (not yet verified) and rejected payments are excluded from every total. Revenue includes both Card payments (card payments) and cash you've logged through the Cash Panel. The amount used is always what you verified landed in your bank, not what the customer was originally asked to pay.

2
Filtering by time period

This Week shows Monday to now. This Month shows the 1st to now. All Time shows everything ever confirmed. Each view gives you the total at the top, then lists every individual payment underneath, newest first. You can see which services each payment was for, whether it was QR or Cash, and when it happened.

3
Revenue vs Profit

Revenue is just the money in — it doesn't subtract your costs. For actual profit (revenue minus rent, bills, commissions, etc.), use the Expense & Profit Tracker in Auto-Manager. Revenue feeds into: the Dashboard stats, the Daily Briefing, the Reports, and the profit calculation.

Reports

Ready-made summaries you can copy and send to anyone
▼
1
What's in a report

Each report gives you: total revenue, number of customers who paid, average spend per customer, how much was QR vs Cash (with percentages), which services made the most money, your busiest day, and how this period compares to the previous one (e.g. this week vs last week — did you go up or down?).

2
The Copy button

Tap "Copy" and it creates a clean text summary you can paste anywhere. Send it to a business partner on WhatsApp, save it in Notes, or email it to your accountant. It's formatted to be readable as plain text without any special apps.

3
Which period to choose

This Week (Monday to now) — partial, updates as the week goes on. Last Week (last Monday to Sunday) — a complete 7-day picture, best for weekly reviews. This Month (1st to now) — useful for monthly check-ins.

Pro Tip

Reports show revenue only, not profit. If you want to know how much you actually kept after expenses and barber commissions, check the Expense & Profit Tracker in Auto-Manager instead.

Bookings

Every appointment customers have booked online
▼
1
Where bookings come from

When a customer fills out the booking form on your website, it saves their name, phone number, email (if they gave one), the service they picked, which barber they want (or "Any Available"), and their chosen date and time. That booking appears here straight away, newest at the top.

2
Bookings and payments are separate things

A booking is an appointment. A payment is money. They're not directly linked — a customer books in one step and pays in another. The system connects them behind the scenes by matching the date and service name. This means:

• A customer can book but not pay yet (they'll pay when they come in)
• A walk-in can pay without ever booking online
• The match is used to auto-assign commissions and send review requests

For these automations to work best, customers should book online before coming in.

3
Bookings automatically build your client database

Every booking adds to the Client Intelligence system. The customer's phone number is used to identify them — so if the same person books three times, you'll see one client profile with 3 visits. Their favourite service, favourite barber, total spend, and visit history all build up automatically without you doing anything.

Important

The available time slots customers see are based on your opening hours in Settings. If a day is marked "Closed", no slots show for that day. If you change your hours, customers who already booked during the old times won't be automatically cancelled — you'd need to contact them yourself.

Staff Management

Add your barbers so everything else works
▼
1
Why you need to add your barbers here

Adding a barber's name does three important things:

• Booking page — customers can pick that barber when booking online
• Commissions — you can set their commission rate and the system auto-calculates what you owe them
• Duty status — they get marked as "on duty" when the shop opens

Without adding barbers here, commissions can't auto-calculate and customers can't request a specific barber.

2
Use consistent names

The commission system works by matching the barber name from a booking to the names in this list. If a customer books with "Mo" but you added the barber as "Mohammed", the system can't match them and the commission goes unassigned. Pick one name per barber (the one customers use most) and stick with it everywhere.

3
Removing a barber

Deleting a barber removes them from future bookings and commission calculations. Their past records (bookings they did, commissions they earned) stay in the system — nothing historical gets deleted. They just won't appear as an option going forward.

Three categories

Uploading photos

Discount Codes

Create codes customers type in to get money off
▼
1
Creating a code

Pick a code name (like "SUMMER20" or "FRIENDS"), choose the discount type, set the value, and set a start and end date. You can also set a max number of uses (or leave it unlimited). Customers type this code on the pay page and the discount is applied to their total automatically. The system checks the code is valid, within dates, and hasn't been used too many times.

2
Percentage vs Fixed

Percentage = a % off the total. So 20% off a £25 haircut = £5 off, customer pays £20.
Fixed = a flat amount off. So £5 off means they always save £5 regardless of the total.

The discount is applied before the customer sees their final price. Your payment records store both the original price and the discounted price so you can always see what the discount cost you.

3
Auto-generate

Don't want to think of a code? Tap "Generate" and it creates a random one like "GOLD4K2M". Or just type whatever you want — it gets automatically capitalised. Customers can enter codes in any case (uppercase or lowercase) and it'll still work.

Important

Deleting a code is permanent. If customers already have it (from flyers, Instagram, etc.), they'll get an "invalid code" error. Safer option: set the end date to today or limit it to 0 uses instead of deleting.

What is Auto-Manager?

Your shop running itself — stock, reviews, profit, commissions, clients
▼
Every time you confirm a payment, all of this happens automatically
You confirm payment
→
Stock ↓ Reviews ↓ Commission ↓
1
The big picture

Auto-Manager is a set of 5 tools that automate the boring parts of running a barbershop. Instead of manually counting stock, chasing Google reviews, calculating barber commissions, tracking expenses, and remembering customer preferences — the system does it for you. All you have to do is confirm payments and log cash like you normally would. Everything else happens in the background.

2
The 5 systems

Inventory — tracks your product stock (gels, sprays, blades) and reduces it automatically when services are paid for.

Reviews — after a payment, queues up a Google review request you can send to the customer via WhatsApp with one tap.

Expenses & Profit — tracks your costs (rent, bills, supplies) and shows your actual profit after everything is subtracted.

Commissions — automatically calculates what each barber earned based on the payments they handled.

Clients — builds a profile for every customer automatically from their bookings — visit history, favourite service, favourite barber, how much they spend.

3
How does the automation actually work?

The moment you press "Confirm" on a payment (or log a cash payment), three things fire at the same time:

• The system checks if any products are linked to the services that were paid for, and reduces the stock count
• It looks for a matching booking from the same day to find the customer's phone number, then queues a review request
• It finds which barber did the job (from the booking) and calculates their commission

All of this takes less than a second. You don't see it happening — it just works.

4
What about walk-ins?

Walk-in customers who didn't book online are the one gap. Since there's no booking to match against, the system can't automatically find the barber's name or the customer's phone number. For walk-ins, you'll need to manually assign the barber (for commissions) and manually enter the phone number (for review requests). Stock tracking still works fine because it's based on what services were paid for, not who booked.

5
Engine Status card

At the top of Auto-Manager. Shows you if everything is running smoothly. "Backfill & Sync" is for when you first set up — it processes all your past bookings to build client profiles and catch up on anything that was missed. "Refresh Metrics" just updates the numbers on the status card. You shouldn't need to use these often — they're mainly for first-time setup or troubleshooting.

Pro Tip

You'll see "Needs ___" banners on some tabs. These tell you about optional upgrades (like WhatsApp Business API for automated sending). Everything works without them — they'd just make it even more hands-off.

Inventory & Stock

Know what products you have and when to reorder
▼
1
Adding a product

For each product you use in the shop (styling gel, beard oil, razor blades, etc.), you add it here with:

• Name — what it is
• Category — Hair Care, Supplies, Equipment, etc.
• Current stock — how many you have right now
• Reorder level — the number where you want to be warned it's running low (e.g. 5 means you get an alert when it drops below 5)
• Cost price — what you paid per unit (so the system can calculate the value of your stock)
• Linked services — which services use this product (e.g. link "Styling Gel" to "Skin Fade")

2
Automatic stock tracking

This is the clever part. When you confirm a payment for "Skin Fade" and you've linked Styling Gel to that service, the stock count goes down by 1 automatically. You get an instant notification if it drops below your reorder level. No more running out of product mid-shift because you forgot to check.

3
The stats at the top

Total Products = how many different products you're tracking. Low Stock = how many are below the reorder level (shows in red when there are any). Stock Value = the total value of everything on your shelves (stock count × what you paid per unit).

4
Manual adjustments

+ / - buttons let you adjust stock by 1 (for corrections). Restock lets you add a bulk amount when a delivery arrives. Delete removes the product entirely. The Stock Log at the bottom records every change — whether it was automatic (from a payment), a restock, or a manual adjustment — so you have a full history.

Important

The service names you link must match what's in your price list. "Skin Fade" will match "skin fade" or "Classic Skin Fade" (not case-sensitive), but won't match just "Fade". If you rename a service in Prices, update it in your linked products too.

Upgrade: Barcode Scanner

Connect a Bluetooth barcode scanner to your phone or tablet. When a delivery arrives, scan each product and the stock count updates instantly — no tapping + buttons one by one. You can also scan products during a stocktake to quickly verify counts against what the system says. Without a scanner, everything still works fine with the manual + / - and Restock buttons — the scanner just makes it faster, especially if you carry a lot of products.

Review Engine

Get more Google reviews without the hassle
▼
How it works from payment to review request
Payment confirmed
→

1
First, set it up

You need to configure a few things once:

• Google Review URL — Go to your Google Business Profile, find the "Ask for reviews" link (it looks like g.page/r/...). Paste it here.
• Message template — Write the WhatsApp message you want to send. Use {name} where the customer's name should go and {link} where the review link should go. Example: "Hi {name}! Thanks for visiting Palace Grooming Room. We'd love a quick review: {link}"
• Auto-queue — Turn this on so every confirmed payment automatically creates a review request
• Minimum spend — Set a minimum (e.g. £15) so small purchases like a single add-on don't trigger a review request

2
How it finds the customer's phone number

When a payment is confirmed, the system looks for a booking from the same day with the same service. If found, it grabs the phone number from that booking. If there's no matching booking (walk-in customer), it shows up as "Walk-in (no contact)" and gives you an "Add Phone" button so you can type it in manually.

3
Sending the review request

Your review queue shows a list of customers waiting. Tap "Send via WhatsApp" and it opens WhatsApp with the message already written and the customer's number filled in — you just tap send. On your phone it opens the WhatsApp app directly. On a computer it opens WhatsApp Web. After sending, the status changes to "Sent". You can also "Skip" customers you don't want to message.

4
Tracking progress

The stats at the top show: how many are waiting to be sent, how many you've sent, how many actually left a review, and how many you skipped. Use "Mark Reviewed" when you spot their review on Google. This gives you a clear picture of your review conversion rate.

Pro Tip

Send review requests within 1–2 hours of the appointment while the experience is fresh. Check the queue at the end of each day and batch-send them all at once.

Upgrade: WhatsApp Business API

Right now, tapping "Send via WhatsApp" opens the WhatsApp app with the message ready — you just hit send. With the WhatsApp Business API connected, the message sends completely automatically the moment a payment is confirmed. No tapping, no opening WhatsApp, no batching at end of day. Every customer gets a review request within seconds of paying, without you lifting a finger. The queue still tracks everything so you can see what was sent and follow up on who hasn't reviewed yet.

Upgrade: Google Business API

Currently, you manually tap "Mark Reviewed" when you spot a customer's review on Google. With the Google Business API connected, the system checks Google automatically and detects when a review comes in. It matches it to the customer who was sent the request and updates their status to "Reviewed" on its own. You also get stats like your average star rating, newest reviews pulled into the dashboard, and the ability to reply to reviews directly from the admin panel.

1
Recurring expenses (bills that repeat)

These are costs that happen regularly — rent, electric, water, insurance, subscriptions, etc. Add each one with the amount and how often it repeats (weekly or monthly). The system automatically adds that expense to your records each month. You'll never forget to account for rent again. You can pause a recurring expense if it stops temporarily (e.g. a subscription you cancelled) without deleting it.

2
One-off expenses

For costs that don't repeat — new clippers, a repair, marketing flyers, a one-time supply purchase, etc. Just log what it was, how much, and what date. Pick a category (Equipment, Repairs, Marketing, Supplies, Other) so you can see where your money goes.

The Profit Card

4

Recurring expense categories: Rent, Utilities, Supplies, Insurance, Software, Other. One-off categories: Equipment, Repairs, Marketing, Supplies, Other. These don't change how profit is calculated — they just help you see at a glance where most of your money is going so you can make smarter decisions.

Important

Recurring expenses are added per calendar month. If you set rent at £2000/month, it adds one £2000 entry each month. Opening the panel multiple times won't create duplicate entries — it only adds once per month. If you change the amount, only future months are affected.

Staff Commissions

Automatically calculates what you owe each barber
▼
1
Setting commission rates

There's a default rate that applies to everyone (starts at 40%). If a barber has a different deal, you can set a custom rate just for them. For example: default is 40%, but Mo gets 50% because he's senior. Changing a rate only affects future payments — past commissions stay as they were.

2
How commissions are auto-assigned

When you confirm a payment, the system figures out which barber did the job automatically. Here's how:

• It looks at the payment date and the services paid for
• It searches for a booking from the same day with a matching service
• If found, it pulls the barber's name from that booking
• It looks up that barber's commission rate
• It calculates: payment amount × rate = commission owed

Example: Customer paid £25 for a Skin Fade. Booking shows Mo did it. Mo's rate is 40%. Commission = £25 × 40% = £10.

3
When it can't auto-assign

Sometimes matching fails: the customer was a walk-in (no booking), the customer chose "Any Available" instead of a specific barber, or the barber name doesn't match the staff list. You'll see an amber warning showing how many payments are "unassigned". Tap on them to manually pick which barber did the job.

4
Monthly summary — paying your barbers

Shows a breakdown for each barber this month: how many customers they served, total revenue from their services, and how much commission they're owed. The total at the bottom is what you need to pay out. Use this at the end of each month (or week, however you pay) to settle up with your team.

Pro Tip

Commissions are automatically subtracted from your profit in the Expense Tracker. If profit looks too high, check for unassigned payments — they count as 0% commission which inflates the number.

Client Intelligence

1
Zero effort required

You don't need to type anything. Every time a customer books online, the system automatically creates or updates their profile using their phone number as the identifier. If "Mo" books today and "Mohammed" books next month with the same phone number, they're treated as the same person. The name updates to whatever they used most recently.

2
What you know about each client

Each profile shows:

• Name and phone — from their bookings
• First visit — when they first booked
• Last visit — when they most recently booked
• Total visits — how many times they've booked
• Total spend — how much they've spent across all visits
• Average spend — their typical spend per visit
• Favourite service — the service they book most (e.g. "Skin Fade" if they've had 5 Skin Fades and 2 Beard Trims)
• Favourite barber — the barber they request most

3
The stats at the top

Total Clients = how many unique customers you have in the system. New This Month = customers who booked for the first time this month (genuinely new faces). Return Rate = percentage of clients who've come back more than once — a high number means great customer loyalty.

4
Finding clients

Use the search box to look up anyone by name or phone number. The main list shows your most loyal customers first (sorted by visit count). Each card has gold tags showing their preferred barber and preferred service. The list shows up to 50 clients at a time — use search to find someone specific.

5
First-time setup

The very first time you open Client Intelligence, it processes all your existing bookings to build profiles in bulk. After that, each new booking updates the relevant profile individually. If you ever think data looks off, use the "Backfill & Sync" button on the Engine Status card to rebuild everything from scratch.

Important

Client profiles only build from online bookings. Walk-in customers who never booked through the website won't have a profile here, even if they paid. To fully track walk-ins, you'd need to collect their name and phone at the chair — which the system doesn't do yet.

Upgrade: Walk-in Identity Capture

Right now, only customers who book online get a client profile because the booking form collects their name and phone. With walk-in capture built in, the Cash Panel gets a quick "Name & Phone" step before logging the payment. Two taps, takes 5 seconds. That walk-in customer then gets a full profile just like an online booker — their visits count, their spend tracks, they get review requests, and their commission assigns to the right barber. This closes the biggest gap in the system: every paying customer becomes a known client, whether they booked online or just walked in off the street.

Promotions

Advertise deals on your website
▼

Create promotions that show on your website — things like "20% off this week" or "Bundle: Fade + Beard for £20". Each promotion has a name, description, a start and end date, and a type (percentage off, fixed amount off, or bundle deal). The system automatically marks them as "active", "scheduled" (starts in the future), or "expired" based on the dates. You can also manually activate or deactivate them.

Important

Promotions are advertising only — they show the deal on your website but don't actually create a discount code. If your promo says "Use code SUMMER20", you need to separately create that code in the Discount Codes section. The promotion tells customers about the deal; the discount code makes it work at checkout.

🕓

Activity Log

A record of everything you've done in the admin panel
▼

Every action you take is recorded here: confirming payments, logging cash, changing prices, adding staff, toggling shop status, and more. Newest entries at the top. Use the filter buttons to show only certain types (e.g. just payments, or just staff changes). The log never deletes old entries — you have a complete history going back to when you first started using the panel.

Good to know

This only logs your actions in the admin panel. Customer activity (booking, paying) creates its own records in Bookings and Payments. The Activity Log is for admin actions — so if you ever need to check when a price was changed, when a barber was added, or when a payment was confirmed, it's all here.

Settings

Opening hours, shop details, and the auto open/close system
▼
1
Opening Hours — they control 3 things

Set your open and close time for each day of the week. Mark days as "Closed" if you don't work that day. These hours control:

• The booking calendar — customers can only book during these times
• The auto shop scheduler — automatically opens your shop at opening time and closes it at closing time
• The "shop closed during hours" alert — warns you on the dashboard if the shop is showing as closed when it should be open

2
The auto open/close system

Every 5 minutes, the admin panel checks whether it's within your opening hours. If it is and the shop is showing as closed, it automatically opens it (and marks staff as on duty). If it's outside hours and the shop is open, it closes it. But it always respects your manual choices — if you manually open or close the shop, the auto system backs off for 4 hours so it doesn't fight you.

3
Business Profile

Your shop name, address, phone number, and email. Used in daily briefings, reports, and any messages that include your shop details. Update these if anything changes.

Pro Tip

Need to stay open late one night? Just manually toggle the shop to "Open". The auto-scheduler won't close you for 4 hours, which is usually more than enough to finish up. No need to change your permanent opening hours for a one-off.
